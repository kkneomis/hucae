Aspiring Leaders Development Institute (ALDI) Program

A Program Presented by
Howard University School of Business Center for Accounting Education and
the National Association of Black Accountants

The Aspiring Leaders Development Institute (ALDI) Program has been developed by the Howard University School of Business Center for Accounting Education. The primary objective of ALDI is to provide a foundation for “the best and the brightest” who aspire to move their careers in the direction of senior and executive level management. Specifically designed for the young African-American Professional – those with four to seven years of experience – this program creates the opportunity for individuals to increase their self-awareness, and understand how knowing oneself will empower them with the ability to better understand and interrelate with others to ensure the development of a winning management style. Using a wide range of tools, the program seeks to allow participants to understand what is necessary to handle situations, and will allow “practice” in handling these situations.

The program objectives are:

  • To strengthen the development of (or the enhancement of) skills to help empower individuals to reach their corporate goals sooner.
  • To allow for the “evolution” of best practices which can provide the tools and techniques that will accelerate one’s ability to succeed
  • To allow for the development of skills based on one’s individual style and avoid the illusion of a “one size fits all” solution
  • To provide this development opportunity from the perspective of being an African American, and allow for an understanding of the impact this will have on any situation

This program focuses on providing insights into individual style and opportunities to explore the impact of this style, how to manage this style, and the impact on developing a management style. It also builds on this information from the standpoint of how style affects team development and dynamics. Other topics discussed include mentoring, effective high performing teams and dealing with difficult team members and clients.

Additionally, it focuses on developing leadership skills – specifically coaching skills. Case studies will be used extensively throughout to allow the participants to practice what they are learning.
